¿What is mead?

Mead (/miːd/), or fermented honey water, is an alcoholic beverage, sometimes with various fruits, spices, grains, or hops. The alcoholic content ranges from about 3.5% ABV to more than 18%. The defining characteristic of mead is that the majority of the beverage's fermentable sugar is derived from honey. It may be still, carbonated, or naturally sparkling; dry, semi-sweet, or sweet.

Ingredients for making mead

  • ¾ cup (236 ml) honey (raw if possible)
  • 3 cups (946 ml) room temperature water
  • ¼ teaspoon (1.2g) Lalvin D47 Saccharomyces cerevisiae (a common and inexpensive wine yeast), or other type of yeast

Steps to prepare mead

  1. Clean equipment with soap and water. If you prefer and have it available, disinfect all equipment with ethanol. However, this step is not necessary. You will be adding such huge quantities of yeast that they will outcompete other microbes.
  2. Rehydrate the dried yeast in 50ml of water at 40-43ºC (104-109ºF). You can make mead with a variety of yeasts: natural yeast from raw honey, champagne yeast, bread yeast… If you are feeling ambitious, make different batches with different yeast varieties and compare the fermentation rate and final flavor.
  3. Mix water and honey in the jar. Stir until the honey fully dissolves.
  4. Add the yeast to the jar. This process is called “pitching the yeast”.
  5. Return the mead to the jar and cork it with an airlock cap or a balloon. You can also very loosely cover it with a traditional mason jar lid, but you will need to check that it remains loose daily, and “burp” the jar to release the pressure so the jar does not shatter.
  6. After 3 or 4 days (more if the room is cold, less if it’s hot), the brew should be bubbly and fragrant. Once it’s bubbly, you can transfer the mead into a clean jar, however this is not necessary. Do not transfer the yeast “sludge” that has developed on the bottom of the jar (it is not unhealthy, but it is also not delicious).
  7. Leave the mead for another 2-4 weeks, until bubbling slows. Make sure to stop the fermentation before the yeast consume all of the sugar. The final product should still be sweet, slightly alcoholic, and preferably have some fizz. Taste the mead every couple of days. If you would like your final mead fizzy, you can seal the jar for the last few days to lock in the carbon dioxide. Be careful with this last step - if you seal your jar in the first 1-2 weeks and there are still many remaining sugars, the yeast will produce more carbon dioxide than the mason jar can contain and the jar will explode.
  8. After 2-4 weeks, your mead is done! Drink it now, or age it.
